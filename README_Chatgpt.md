## 🔬 About ONC

Ocean Networks Canada (ONC), an initiative of the University of Victoria, operates one of the most advanced ocean observatory systems in the world.

Our infrastructure spans **Canada’s Pacific, Arctic, and Atlantic coasts**, delivering continuous, high-resolution data streams from the seafloor, water column, and coastal environments.

- 🌐 800+ km of powered fibre-optic subsea cables  
- 📡 Hundreds of instruments and 2,000+ sensors  
- 📊 200+ GB of data generated daily  
- 🌍 Open, real-time access to global users  

These observatories enable long-term monitoring of complex Earth systems across **biological, chemical, geological, and physical domains**.

---

## ⚙️ Data Infrastructure & Systems

ONC operates **Internet-connected observatories** that function as persistent, distributed sensing platforms.

### Core Systems

- **NEPTUNE** — regional-scale deep-sea observatory in the northeast Pacific  
- **VENUS** — coastal observatory in the Salish Sea  
- Community observatories across Arctic and coastal regions  

Unlike traditional ship-based sampling, ONC systems provide:
- Continuous time-series data (years to decades)  
- Remote experiment control and sensor reconfiguration  
- Real-time streaming via high-bandwidth subsea networks

## 📊 For Data Scientists

ONC is fundamentally a **big data + Earth systems platform**.

### Data Characteristics

- Streaming + historical datasets  
- Multimodal (time series, imagery, acoustics, geospatial)  
- High-frequency sampling  
- Long-duration continuity  
- Standardized formats (NetCDF, APIs, CF conventions)  

### Typical Workflows

- Time-series analysis & anomaly detection  
- Signal processing (acoustic / seismic)  
- Spatiotemporal modeling  
- Machine learning for classification & forecasting  
- Data fusion across heterogeneous sensor networks
